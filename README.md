# Catan: The Forgotten Temples

**A 3D-Printable Expansion for The Settlers of Catan**

> *The island was not uninhabited when we arrived. Ancient stone structures loom over the richest lands, cursing the soil. Before we can harvest the bounty of Catan, we must clear the ruins of the old gods.*

## üìñ Overview

**The Forgotten Temples** is a fan-made expansion that introduces verticality and archeology to the classic game. It utilizes 3D-printed "Ruin" pieces to block high-value resource tiles (6s and 8s) at the start of the game, forcing players to balance early expansion against expensive excavation efforts.

This expansion creates a "high-risk, high-reward" economy and solves the "runaway leader" problem by making the best spots on the board require significant investment to unlock.

## üèóÔ∏è 3D Printing The Components

### The Temple Ruin Variants

There are two 3D models available for this expansion. Both are designed to sit directly on top of the cardboard number token.

* **Temple 1:** Custom generated model.
* **Temple 2:** Based on a community design (see Attribution).

* **Quantity:** 4 to 6 pieces (enough to cover all Red Numbers).
* **Design Constraints:**
  * **Shape:** Cylindrical or Hexagonal ring.
  * **Inner Diameter:** ~29mm (Must fit *over* a standard Catan number token so the token sits inside).
  * **Height:** ~20mm (Tall enough to look imposing, short enough to see the board).
  * **Visibility:** The design must feature arches, windows, or a hollow top so players can clearly read the number (6 or 8) underneath.
  * **Base:** Thin footprint to ensure it does not crowd the settlements at the intersections.

### Recommended Print Settings

* **Material:** PLA (Stone Grey or Moss Green).
* **Infill:** 15-20% (Standard).
* **Supports:** Minimal (Design dependent).

## üìú The Ruleset

### 1. Setup

1. Construct the board (Hexes and Ports) as usual.
2. Place Number Tokens as usual.
3. **Placement:** Place a **Temple Ruin** 3D piece on every **Red Number (6 and 8)**.
    * *Variant:* For a chaotic game, place Ruins on random tiles before numbers are revealed.

### 2. The Curse (Passive Effect)

A Hex containing a Temple Ruin is considered **Dormant**.

* **Zero Production:** Even if the number is rolled, this Hex produces **NO resources** for any player.
* **Sanctuary:** The Robber **cannot** be placed on a Hex that contains a Ruin.

### 3. Excavation (Active Gameplay)

To remove a Ruin, a player must have a **Settlement**, **City**, or **Active Knight** connected to the Hex. On their turn, they may perform one of the following actions.

**The Base Cost:** 1 Ore + 1 Brick + 1 Wood.

| Method | Requirement | Cost | The Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Manual Excavation** | Connected **Settlement** | **1 Ore, 1 Brick, 1 Wood** | **"The Archaeologist's Loot"**<br>1. Remove Ruin to box & Unlock Hex.<br>2. **Roll a die:**<br>‚Ä¢ **1-3:** Take **1 Resource** card of that hex type.<br>‚Ä¢ **4-6:** Take **2 Resource** cards of that hex type. |
| **Royal Restoration** | Connected **City** | **1 Ore, 1 Brick, 1 Wood** | **"The Prestige"**<br>1. Hex Unlocks.<br>2. **Player keeps the Ruin.**<br>3. It counts as **1 Permanent Victory Point.** |
| **Military Purge** *(C&K)* | Active **Strong Knight** (Lvl 2+) | **Deactivate Knight** | **"The Efficient Command"**<br>1. Remove Ruin to box & Unlock Hex.<br>2. **No Resource Reward.**<br>*(The reward is saving your Ore/Wood/Brick cards).* |

### 4. Strategic Notes

* **The Greed Gap:** Players with Cities are the only ones who can gain Victory Points from Ruins. A player may refuse to clear a Ruin with a Settlement, hoping to upgrade to a City first to secure the point.
* **The Cost:** The cost includes Road materials (Brick/Wood). This forces a choice: Expand your road network, or fix your production engine?

## ‚öîÔ∏è Compatibility

### Cities & Knights

* **Fully Compatible.**
* The **Military Purge** rule gives Knights a non-combat utility, allowing players to save resources by using their army to clear land.
* *Note:* Basic Knights (Level 1) are too weak to clear Ruins.

### Seafarers

* **Fully Compatible.**
* Ruins can be placed on Gold Fields or standard resource hexes on foreign islands.
* *Optional Rule:* If a Ruin is on a Gold Field, the clearing cost is **2 Ore + 2 Wood** (representing a sunken/buried treasure chest).
